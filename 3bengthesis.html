Heathrow Net-Zero Water Blueprint

A three-pillar water management blueprint for Heathrow Airport, designed in the GreenSky Infrastructure consultancy brief to support the airport's 2030 net-zero target and the UK's Jet Zero strategy. The system couples a subsurface-flow stormwater wetland, a closed-loop automatic de-icing facility, and Taguchi/ABAQUS-optimised cooling towers — together capturing ~83,000 m³/yr of non-potable water and offsetting ~1,346 tCO₂e/yr across operations.

§ 02 / Pillar I · Subsurface Wetland

1,680 m² · 2.20 m depth · 3,700 m³

An engineered subsurface-flow wetland sized from a rational-method runoff calculation and a first-order plug-flow pollutant model (Kadlec & Wallace, 2009). Heathrow's 700 mm/yr precipitation across a 10-hectare catchment yields 56,000 m³/yr inflow, sized for both 90% TSS and 70% nutrient removal targets:

  • Surface area — 1,680 m² (larger of TSS / nutrient-driven sizings) at 2.20 m depth → 3,700 m³ working volume.
  • Geometry — 2:1 length-to-width rectangle (58 × 29 m) for uniform plug-flow distribution.
  • HLR — 0.09 m/day, within the 0.05–0.1 m/day sweet spot for contact-time and clog prevention.
  • Substrate stack — Macadam gravel (80%) + Birch biochar (20%) at 1.20 m thickness, atop transition pea-gravel and HDPE drainage layer separated by Tencate Mirafi FW402 woven geotextile (52.5 kN/m).
  • Vegetation — Phragmites australis, Typha latifolia, Iris pseudacorus — UK-native species per Wildlife & Countryside Act 1981, Schedule 9.

§ 03 / Pillar II · Closed-Loop De-Icing

73 × 43 × 20 m · 62,780 m³ hangar
Fig. 03-A · Closed-loop de-icing facility · plan view

A purpose-built hangar housing the Airbus A320 family with six 6-axis robotic arms and an automated glycol recovery loop. Sized at 62,780 m³ — comparable to AvinXT's 48,000 m³ Geneva facility but scaled for Heathrow's 44% A320 inventory share.

  • Recovery — 95% glycol recycle year-round → 40% reduction in propylene glycol imports.
  • Operations — six Kuka 50 kW arms, 8 hr/day, ~50 washes/day = 911,040 kWh/yr robotic energy.
  • Roof harvest — 45 PowerNest monocrystalline panels (11.93 kW) + 6 vertical-axis turbines (3 kW) integrate the facility into the wider GSI roof workstream.
  • Seasonal cycle — demand peaks Nov–Mar; suspension Apr–Oct stores 500 m³ glycol for high-demand periods.
  • Fuel co-benefit — consistent de-icing saves 17.10 kg fuel/A320 flight × 3 sectors/day → 59,169 kgCO₂ offset per aircraft annually.

§ 04 / Pillar III · Cooling Tower Tuning

Taguchi L27 · ABAQUS CFD · algorithmic only

Heathrow's cooling towers account for 10–15% of annual airport water consumption. A purely algorithmic optimisation — no new construction, hence zero embodied carbon — identifies the operational sweet-spot via Taguchi L27 design of experiments and ABAQUS CFD simulation across 100 iterations.

  • Variables — inlet air rate, water flow rate, fill porosity (the three parameters with highest thermal-performance impact).
  • Optimum — fill porosity 60%, concentration cycles 8 (vs. typical 4–6) → ~45 m³ make-up water saved per tower per day.
  • Outcome — 12% airport-wide cooling water reduction, +35 tCO₂e/yr offset, with no capex beyond control-system tuning.
