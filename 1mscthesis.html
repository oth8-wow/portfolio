Lean Direct Injection
for Hydrogen Combustors.

An atmospheric experimental campaign isolating how injector-to-plane spacing governs near-field mixing, flame topology, and NOₓ formation in a 100% hydrogen combustor. Three spacings (h/D = 6.0, 8.5, 10.0) tested across two bulk velocities and a global equivalence ratio sweep from φg = 0.1 to 0.6, stabilised by a twelve-port pilot array that extended the lean window far below previous campaigns on the same rig.

Five synchronised diagnostics — OH*-Chemiluminescence, OH-PLIF, LIBS, PIV, and dynamic pressure — resolving the mixing/topology/emissions triad against a single geometric variable.

§ 01 / Design Variable

One geometry, three spacings, one matrix

Hydrogen's flame speed (2.75 m/s), broad flammability (4.0–75.0% by volume), and 0.02 mJ ignition energy collapse the safe operating window of every premixed combustor architecture inherited from natural gas. Lean Direct Injection sidesteps the problem by injecting fuel directly into the reaction zone, holding globally lean conditions, and shortening residence at high temperature. The remaining design question is geometric.

This study isolates a single variable: the distance from injector to dump plane, expressed non-dimensionally as h/D. Three plates were machined — SB1, SB2, SB3 at h/D = 6.0, 8.5, and 10.0. Twelve circumferential pilot ports stabilise the array down to ultra-lean conditions earlier campaigns on this rig could not reach. Every other variable is fixed.

§ 03 / Results

SB1 vs SB2 vs SB3 across the lean corridor
Fig. B · NOₓ at 15% O₂ across the lean corridor · Um = 80 m/s · rebuilt from Table IV

The geometries separate cleanly. SB2, the intermediate spacing, outperforms both extremes across the diagnostic suite: lowest NOₓ across the corridor, the most compact and well-anchored OH* probability fields at mid-lean conditions, and the lowest LIBS variability at under 8% versus roughly 15% for SB1 and SB3. Closer (SB1) elongates the flame and steepens shear; further (SB3) broadens the footprint and weakens the core. SB2 balances penetration and confinement.

As mixtures strengthen toward φg = 0.6, the three geometries converge: OH* fields collapse to near-identical ovoid shapes, NOₓ differences narrow, and PIV shear-layer differences become second-order for flame presence. Geometry dominates near the lean stability limit. Fuel availability dominates above it. That transition is the design margin.

§ 05 / Honest Margins

What's bounded · what comes next

§ Limitations

PIVlab's mapping assumptions and obstructive TiO₂ seeding required manual cleaning. Final velocity contours retain some residual non-smoothness that may mask fine shear-layer detail.

Low-level optical scatter and intensifier grain persisted in OH* and PLIF frames despite flame-off subtraction. Verification shows these processing choices do not bias the trends, but presentation quality is affected.

All testing is atmospheric and confined to one optical chamber. Pressure scaling for hydrogen is non-trivial and acoustic mode structure differs at engine conditions. Conclusions stand as validated atmospheric baselines.

§ Future Work

Re-run the matrix at elevated temperature and pressure (preheated air) to quantify NOₓ and stability at engine-relevant conditions. Link atmospheric and pressurised maps through Da and Re scaling.

Vary orifice diameter and injection angle to span the MMX-relevant momentum-flux range (J ≈ 3–4) at φ ≈ 0.3–0.4. Connect single-nozzle LDI baseline to MMX array design rules.

Upgrade 2D/2C PIV to time-resolved Tomo-PIV for true 3D fields. Pair OH-PLIF with CH₂O- and NO-PLIF to distinguish preheating chemistry from main reaction zones and locate post-flame NOₓ formation directly.
