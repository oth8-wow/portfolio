Deming Corridor
Solar Farm.

A comprehensive outline design study for a 250 GWh/year utility-scale photovoltaic facility in south-western New Mexico — covering site selection via GIS analysis, system specification, intermittency management, environmental impact, and full CAPEX/OPEX financial modelling.

The farm deploys 328,948 Heliene 72M monocrystalline modules on fixed-tilt racking across 1.74 km², positioned near existing high-voltage infrastructure to minimise grid connection costs while maximising output under New Mexico's 7.50 kWh/m² daily irradiance.

§ 01 / Site Selection

32°09′07″N, 107°57′11″W · Luna County

Site selection combined RE-Powering Mapper 3.0 with Google Earth to identify a rectangular plot in Luna County, New Mexico, NW of Gage and east of Sunshine. The region receives 7.50 kWh/m² daily at an elevation of 1,317 m, with terrain slope below 2%, eliminating land grading. Sandy and loamy soil composition further reduces civil engineering costs.

Proximity to high-voltage infrastructure was a primary criterion. The Luna Energy Facility (650 MW) sits 15.46 km away; the Pyramid Generating Station (186 MW) is 7.60 km distant. Both allow shorter transmission line installations, directly reducing grid connection CAPEX. The mapped high-voltage network, sourced via Open Infrastructure Map, provides direct access to New Mexico's energy grid.

The farm's final adjusted area of 1.74 km² incorporates a 35% buffer zone for drainage, security fencing, and future expansion — structured into 10 columns of 150 rows each, with 15-metre buffer paths between columns for maintenance access.

§ 04 / Financial Model

15-year operational horizon · LCOE 0.0218 GBP/kWh

Total CAPEX of £276.55 million reflects detailed cost integration across land acquisition, grid connection (£8.45 million for 15.5 km HV transmission), substation and legal fees, and full mechanical and electrical installation. The dominant cost driver is installation at £217.25 million, priced at 0.604 GBP/W using ENVERUS regional templates for the U.S. Southwest.

Module procurement totals £48.92 million for 328,948 Heliene units at £148.73 per module. The 125 inverters and 25 transformers contribute a combined £773,075 — a modest share given their critical role in system efficiency.

Over 15 years, OPEX totals £29.63 million, equating to 0.0218 GBP/kWh. Against typical fossil fuel LCOEs exceeding £0.04/kWh, the farm delivers both cost stability and regulatory compliance with the Energy Transition Act.

The decision to use fixed-tilt racking over two-axis tracking was financially driven: tracking systems cost $600–$1,000 per panel for a maximum 90 W boost per module. On New Mexico's abundant, affordable land, deploying additional fixed-tilt modules is demonstrably more economical.

§ 05 / Intermittency & Grid Integration

Storage · dispatch · demand-side

Intermittency is addressed through a layered storage and demand-side strategy. A 500 MWh lithium-ion BESS — 129 Tesla Megapack units or 67 Fluence Grid-Stack units — with 125 MW discharge capacity over a 4-hour duration stores excess daytime generation and dispatches at night, achieving 90%+ round-trip efficiency. This prevents curtailment and enhances grid frequency stability.

For seasonal storage, Elephant Butte Reservoir is identified as a candidate for retrofitting into a 2 GWh pumped hydro system with a 500 MW turbine, providing 10+ hours of backup power during prolonged low-irradiance periods. The Hornsdale Power Reserve in South Australia serves as a validated precedent for BESS effectiveness at grid scale.

Demand-side optimisation uses a midday tariff of $0.03/kWh, mirroring Arizona's pricing structure, to shift industrial loads toward peak solar hours. HVDC transmission enables long-distance export with 40% lower losses than AC lines, extending the farm's effective service area beyond Luna County.
